What to Pack For: May Weather on the Crest

The route spends 7 of 8 nights at 4,500–5,920 ft and tops out at Kuwohi (Clingmans Dome, 6,643 ft). Plan for temperatures about 18–20°F cooler than Gatlinburg, high humidity, and the likelihood of rain on 1 day in 3. Plan for the cold-snap, not the average. Mount LeConte has seen snow in mid-May and lows in the low 20s°F. A 20°F-rated sleeping bag and a full rain shell (jacket + pants) are non-negotiable, even though the forecast will probably look benign.

Core Essentials

Pack, poles, permit, and the non-negotiables.

🏗 Pack & Carry

  • Backpack, 50–65L
  • Pack liner (trash-compactor bag) primary waterproofing — trust this more than a pack cover
  • Pack cover secondary, good against abrasion
  • Trekking poles big help on the ~17,000 ft of descent
  • Dry sacks / stuff sacks one for sleep clothes, one for food

📝 Permits & Docs

  • Printed backcountry permit every shelter listed
  • Printed parking tag each vehicle, dashboard
  • Driver’s license / ID
  • Cash & one credit card
  • Emergency contact card plus Backcountry Office: 865-436-1297
  • Car key (and a second hidden on the Fontana car)

Sleep System

Shelters only — no tent needed. Shelters are three-sided, open to the wind, with a raised platform floor. Pack as if you're sleeping outside at 40°F with the chance of 25°F.

💤 Sleeping

  • Sleeping bag, 20°F comfort rating synthetic or DWR-treated down; damp crest air kills untreated down
  • Sleeping pad, R-value 3+ inflatable or CCF; shelter platforms are hard
  • Small inflatable pillow or a stuff sack filled with your puffy
  • Sleep socks (wool, dedicated, dry)
  • Sleep base layer top & bottom keep dry — never hike in these
  • Earplugs shelters get loud; mice and snorers
  • Eye mask (optional)

Cook Gear & Water

Springs are located near every shelter on the route but all water must be treated. Stove meals happen at the shelter only.

🔥 Stove & Cookware

  • Canister stove MSR PocketRocket, Soto Windmaster, etc.
  • Fuel canister, 230g (plus a backup 110g) 8 dinners + hot drinks = ~1.5 canisters
  • Pot, 750–900 mL with lid
  • Long-handled spoon or spork
  • Lighter, + backup mini Bic in a Ziploc
  • Windscreen / pot cozy (optional, speeds boils in wind)
  • Dish rag / small sponge
  • Dr. Bronner’s or biodegradable soap, 1 oz

💦 Water

  • Water filter Sawyer Squeeze or Katadyn BeFree
  • 2 L carrying capacity e.g., 1L hard bottle + 1L or 2L soft flask
  • Backup chemical treatment Aquamira drops or tablets
  • Electrolyte mix / LMNT / Nuun 6–8 servings; helps in humidity

🧯 Food Storage

  • Food bag / Ursack or stuff sack every shelter has bear cables — use them nightly
  • 25–30 ft of cord hanging items on cable; backup if the cable is broken
  • Odor-proof liner (OPSak) (optional, recommended)
  • Gallon Ziplocs × 3 trash, smellable repacking, wet items

Rain Gear & Clothing

Rain gear is mandatory — afternoon squalls are routine and the crest stays wet in fog. Layer so you can be warm at 25°F camp and cool on a 70°F climb.

☁ Rain Shell (required)

  • Rain jacket, waterproof hooded shell not a windbreaker — 6+ in. of rain is possible in a week
  • Rain pants also double as wind pants on exposed balds
  • Ball cap / brimmed hat keeps rain off glasses under the hood

🕵 Insulation

  • Puffy jacket synthetic or treated down, ~60g fill
  • Fleece or grid midlayer warm layer that still works wet
  • Warm beanie
  • Light gloves / liner gloves cold wet mornings on the ridge
  • Buff / neck gaiter

🕵️ Hiking Clothes

  • Hiking shirt × 2 one sun-hoody long sleeve, one short sleeve
  • Hiking pants or convertibles ticks are active in May — pants help
  • Hiking shorts (optional)
  • Underwear × 2–3 synthetic or merino
  • Sports bra (if applicable) × 2
  • Hiking socks, wool, × 3 pairs rotate, dry on pack
  • Sun hat or cap

👟 Footwear

  • Trail runners or boots broken in — May trail is muddy
  • Gaiters (optional) keep mud and small stones out
  • Camp shoes (optional) Crocs or Xero sandals; lets boots dry
  • Moleskin / Leukotape blister prevention — apply early

Navigation, Safety & Personal

Most shelters have a privy. Cell service is patchy on the crest. Assume you’ll need to self-rescue or wait hours for help above 5,000 ft.

📍 Navigation

  • Map: NatGeo #229 Great Smoky Mountains waterproof paper
  • AT Guide or FarOut app (Smokies section) download offline maps before leaving home
  • Compass
  • Printed itinerary mileage, shelter locations, water sources

💡 Light & Power

  • Headlamp fresh batteries or full charge
  • Extra batteries or backup light
  • Phone, with offline maps loaded
  • Power bank, 10,000 mAh enough for 8 days of moderate use
  • Phone charging cable + wall brick (left in car)

💊 First Aid & Repair

  • First aid kit: bandaids, gauze, antiseptic wipes, tweezers
  • Blister kit: Leukotape, moleskin, Compeed
  • Ibuprofen, acetaminophen, Benadryl
  • Any prescription meds (+1 extra day)
  • Anti-diarrheal (Imodium)
  • Tick key or fine-tip tweezers ticks out in May below 4,000 ft
  • Duct tape wrap 3 ft around a trekking pole
  • Tenacious Tape / gear repair patch
  • Spare buckle or cord lock (optional)

🛡 Safety

  • Whistle 3 blasts = distress
  • Knife or multi-tool
  • Emergency bivy or space blanket
  • Fire starter Vaseline cotton balls or stormproof matches
  • Satellite communicator (Garmin inReach, optional but recommended)

🩸 Toiletries

  • Toothbrush (cut handle) + small toothpaste
  • Floss picks
  • Toilet paper, ~1/2 roll in Ziploc
  • Trowel shelters have privies, but carry in case
  • Hand sanitizer, 2 oz
  • Sunscreen, 2 oz sun is strong at 6,000 ft even under haze
  • Lip balm with SPF
  • Bug spray / permethrin on clothes ahead of trip ticks are active
  • Body wipes, ~8 (optional but nice)
  • Small quick-dry towel / bandana
  • Pee rag / funnel (if applicable)

🐤 Nice to Have

  • Small journal + pen
  • Book / Kindle
  • Playing cards
  • Binoculars, compact (optional)
  • Trash bag / ziplock for packed-out trash
  • Clean clothes & shoes in the car at Fontana future you will be very grateful

Repack tip: pull everything out of boxes. Combine oatmeal packets into one Ziploc. Label dinner Ziplocs with the day and any add-ins. You’ll shed ~1/3 of the volume before leaving home.

Bear country: every food item, wrapper, toothpaste, sunscreen, and lip balm goes on the bear cable every night — no exceptions. Pack out all food waste, including fruit peels and noodle broth.
