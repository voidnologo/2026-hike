**Off-trail shelter note:** When stopping at an off-trail shelter, add the round-trip
distance to your daily mileage. Pecks Corner and Mt. Collins each add 1.0 mi round trip.
Spence Field adds 0.4 mi round trip. This matters most on Day 5 if you were to stop at
Mt. Collins (you aren't in this itinerary, but worth noting for adjustments).

**Closely-spaced shelters:** Double Spring Gap and Silers Bald are only 1.7 mi apart.
Russell Field and Mollies Ridge are only 2.5 mi apart. Spence Field to Russell Field
is 2.9 mi. These clusters give flexibility for adjusting daily mileage if needed, but
you can't stay at consecutive-night-restricted shelters back-to-back anyway.

## Shelter Details

### Davenport Gap Shelter (Night 0)
- **Capacity:** 12 bunks (two-level wooden platform)
- **Built:** 1961, renovated 1998
- **Maintained by:** Smoky Mountain Hiking Club
- **Water:** Spring nearby
- **Privy:** No privy at this shelter
- **Notes:** 0.9 mi from the trailhead. Easy afternoon walk. The shelter is in
  reasonable condition but basic. Being the first shelter from the NE boundary,
  it sees a mix of section hikers and thru-hikers.

### Cosby Knob Shelter (Night 1)
- **Capacity:** 12 bunks
- **Water:** Spring nearby (reliable in May)
- **Privy:** Yes
- **Notes:** Located ~150 ft off the AT on a short side trail. Generally quieter
  than shelters closer to road access points. Good first-night shelter for settling
  into trail rhythm.

### Tricorner Knob Shelter (Night 2)
- **Capacity:** 12 bunks
- **Water:** Spring nearby
- **Privy:** Yes
- **Notes:** ~100 yards off trail (negligible detour). One of the more remote-feeling
  shelters on this stretch. High-elevation location means cooler temps and possible
  wind. Spruce-fir forest setting.

### Pecks Corner Shelter (Night 3)
- **Capacity:** 12 bunks
- **Water:** Spring nearby
- **Privy:** Yes
- **Notes:** 0.5 mi off the AT via Hughes Ridge Trail (1.0 mi round trip). Quieter
  shelter. The walk in is on a well-maintained side trail. Good spot for a rest day
  given the short mileage.

### Icewater Spring Shelter (Night 4)
- **Capacity:** 12 bunks
- **Water:** Icewater Spring (reliable, cold, and close -- one of the better water
  sources on the route)
- **Privy:** Yes
- **Notes:** One of the most crowded shelters in the park due to proximity to
  Newfound Gap road crossing. **Restricted to long-distance hikers only from
  March 15 through June 15** (see detailed section below). Popular with day-hikers
  who sometimes wander through. Despite the crowds, the spring is excellent.

### Mt. Collins Shelter (pass-through on Day 5)
- **Capacity:** 12 bunks
- **Water:** Spring nearby
- **Privy:** Yes
- **Notes:** 0.5 mi off AT on Sugarland Mountain Trail (1.0 mi round trip). Popular
  due to proximity to Newfound Gap and Clingmans Dome. Not a stop on this itinerary,
  but available as a backup if Day 5 needs to be shortened. If you stopped here, you'd
  split Day 5 into two ~7-mi days (Icewater→Collins, Collins→Double Spring Gap).

### Double Spring Gap Shelter (Night 5)
- **Capacity:** 12 bunks
- **Water:** Two springs nearby (hence the name)
- **Privy:** Yes
- **Notes:** On the AT, no detour needed. Sits at ~5,505 ft in a gap between
  higher ridges. Only 1.7 mi from Silers Bald Shelter, so these two are essentially
  interchangeable if one is booked. The two springs are generally reliable in May.

### Silers Bald Shelter (pass-through on Day 6)
- **Capacity:** 12 bunks
- **Water:** Spring nearby
- **Privy:** Yes
- **Notes:** Only 1.7 mi from Double Spring Gap. Available as an alternate Night 5
  stop if Double Spring Gap is full when booking. Named for the bald nearby, though
  the bald has partially grown in over the decades.

### Derrick Knob Shelter (Night 6)
- **Capacity:** 12 bunks
- **Water:** Spring nearby
- **Privy:** Yes
- **Notes:** Sits at ~4,880 ft in a pleasant gap. Named after a moonshiner from the
  area's pre-park history. Central location on the traverse. Good recovery stop after
  the big Day 5.

### Spence Field Shelter (pass-through on Day 7)
- **Capacity:** 12 bunks
- **Water:** Spring nearby
- **Privy:** Yes
- **Notes:** 0.2 mi off AT on Eagle Creek Trail (0.4 mi round trip). Very popular
  in spring for the nearby bald views (Thunderhead Mountain, Rocky Top). If you wanted
  to split Day 7 into two shorter days, you could stop here (6.3 mi from Derrick Knob)
  and then do Russell Field → Mollies Ridge → next day. But that creates two very short
  days (6.3 + 5.4) which doesn't use the schedule efficiently.

### Russell Field Shelter (pass-through on Day 7)
- **Capacity:** 12 bunks
- **Water:** Spring nearby
- **Privy:** Yes
- **Notes:** On the AT. 2.9 mi past Spence Field and 2.5 mi before Mollies Ridge.
  In the heart of the bald/meadow country. Available as an alternate stop if Mollies
  Ridge is booked, but then your final day becomes 13.1 mi to Fontana (still doable
  but a longer finish).

### Mollies Ridge Shelter (Night 7)
- **Capacity:** 12 bunks
- **Water:** Spring nearby
- **Privy:** Yes
- **Notes:** Westernmost AT shelter before the final descent. At 4,570 ft, you're
  still at elevation but the descending trend is clear. 10.6 mi to Fontana Dam from
  here -- a comfortable final day mostly downhill.

## Permit & Booking System

### General Backcountry Permit
- Required for all overnight stays in the backcountry
- Must specify exact shelters for each night
- No changes after booking without calling the Backcountry Office
- Print the paper permit and carry it with you
- Maximum party size: 8 without special approval
- No consecutive nights at the same shelter or at Campsite #113

### Booking Systems

**Old system (before April 29, 2026):**
- URL: https://smokiespermits.nps.gov/
- Can search by date and build night-by-night itinerary
- Shows real-time availability
- This is the system to use if booking before April 29

**New system (after April 30, 2026):**
- URL: https://www.recreation.gov/permits/4675347
- Similar process, different interface
- Availability shown when selecting dates and sites

**Strategy:** Book as early as possible. Have 1-2 alternate shelters per night ready
(e.g., swap Double Spring Gap for Silers Bald, swap Mollies Ridge for Russell Field).
Weekday nights generally have better availability than weekends.

### General Permit vs Thru-Hiker Permit
- **General Backcountry Permit:** What your group needs. Gives you reserved bunks at
  specific shelters on specific nights. You have priority for your reserved space.
- **Thru-Hiker Permit (AT only):** $44.50 flat fee for qualifying AT thru-hikers.
  They must stay in shelters if space is available, but can tent nearby if full. They
  can be displaced by reserved parties like yours.
- **During peak season (Mar 15 - Jun 15):** Four spaces at each AT shelter are reserved
  for thru-hikers. This reduces general permit availability slightly.

## Icewater Spring Restriction

Icewater Spring Shelter is the most crowded AT shelter in GSMNP due to its proximity
to Newfound Gap (the only road crossing on the AT in the Smokies). To manage overuse:

**Restriction:** From March 15 through June 15, Icewater Spring Shelter is restricted
to long-distance hikers only.

**Does your group qualify?** Most likely yes. The NPS defines long-distance hikers as
those hiking through a substantial portion of the park on the AT. A 72-mile full
traverse from Davenport Gap to Fontana Dam clearly qualifies. However:

- **Confirm when booking.** Call the Backcountry Office (865-436-1297) and explicitly
  ask whether your General Backcountry Permit group doing the full 72-mile traverse
  qualifies for Icewater Spring during the restriction period.
- **If they say no:** The fallback is to go Pecks Corner → Mt. Collins in one day
  (14.9 mi, skipping Icewater Spring entirely). This is a long day but doable for
  experienced hikers. It reshuffles the itinerary significantly:

  | Day | Segment | Miles |
  |-----|---------|-------|
  | 3 | Tricorner → Pecks Corner | 5.2 |
  | 4 | Pecks Corner → Mt. Collins | 14.9 (big day) |
  | 5 | Mt. Collins → Derrick Knob | 13.5 |
  | 6-8 | (same as current Days 6-8) | ... |

  This is less ideal because it stacks two ~14-mi days back to back.

## Birch Spring Gap Status

The original Birch Spring Gap Shelter has been **demolished**. The site is now
**Backcountry Campsite #113** -- the only tent-only backcountry site on the AT in
GSMNP (all other AT camping sites are shelters).

- Location: ~5.3 mi south of Mollies Ridge, ~5.3 mi north of Fontana Dam
- Elevation: ~3,834 ft
- Water: Spring at the gap
- The consecutive-night rule applies to Campsite #113 as well

This itinerary skips it entirely. Mollies Ridge to Fontana Dam (10.6 mi) is a
comfortable single-day finish. However, if someone in the group is struggling and
needs to split the final day, Campsite #113 exists as an option -- you'd just need
to have it on your permit.

## Side Trails & Detours

### Mt. Cammerer Fire Tower (near Day 1)
- **Spur trail:** 0.6 mi each way from the AT junction
- **Location:** Between Davenport Gap Shelter and Cosby Knob Shelter
- **Elevation:** ~5,000 ft at the tower
- **Worth it?** Yes if weather is clear. The stone fire tower (built 1937 by the CCC)
  offers 360-degree panoramic views. One of the best viewpoints in the northern
  Smokies. Adds 1.2 mi round trip to Day 1.
- **Note:** The junction is closer to the Cosby Knob end of Day 1, so you'd hit it
  later in the day. Consider energy levels and weather.

### Kuwohi / Clingmans Dome Tower (Day 5)
- **Spur trail:** ~0.5 mi round trip from the AT
- **Elevation:** 6,643 ft (highest point on the entire AT)
- **Worth it?** Absolutely. The concrete observation tower gives panoramic views on
  clear days. This is THE highlight of the traverse. Even in fog, it's worth visiting
  for the milestone.
- **Timing:** You'll pass this on Day 5 (Icewater Spring to Double Spring Gap).
  It's roughly in the first third of the day, between Mt. Collins Shelter and the
  summit. Plan to linger here.
- **Crowds:** The tower is also accessible by car via Clingmans Dome Road from
  Newfound Gap. Expect tourists. The paved path from the parking lot is 0.5 mi;
  you'll approach from the AT side which is less crowded.

### Shuckstack Fire Tower (Day 8)
- **Spur trail:** Short detour (~0.1-0.2 mi) from the AT
- **Location:** Between Birch Spring Gap and Fontana Dam, on the final descent
- **Elevation:** ~4,020 ft
- **Worth it?** Good views of Fontana Lake if weather is clear. Quick detour on
  your final day. The tower itself is a steel structure -- climb at your own risk
  (the stairs are functional but aging).

### Charlies Bunion (near Day 4)
- **Location:** Near Icewater Spring Shelter, slightly off the AT
- **Notes:** Rocky outcrop with dramatic views. Very popular day-hike destination
  from Newfound Gap. If weather is clear on Day 4, it's worth the minimal detour.
  Ask at the shelter for exact directions -- it's close.

## Alternate Itineraries

### If You Need to Shorten to 7 Hiking Days
Drop the rest day by combining Days 3 and 4:

| Day | Segment | Miles |
|-----|---------|-------|
| 1 | DG Shelter → Cosby Knob | 7.1 |
| 2 | Cosby Knob → Tricorner Knob | 7.7 |
| 3 | Tricorner Knob → Icewater Spring | 12.6 (combines old Days 3+4) |
| 4 | Icewater Spring → Double Spring Gap | 13.8 |
| 5 | Double Spring Gap → Derrick Knob | 7.2 |
| 6 | Derrick Knob → Mollies Ridge | 11.7 |
| 7 | Mollies Ridge → Fontana Dam | 10.6 |

This eliminates the Pecks Corner stop and has two consecutive ~12-14 mi days
(Days 3-4). Doable but less comfortable.

### If Someone Is Struggling and You Need a Slower Pace
Split Day 5 by adding a Mt. Collins stop:

| Day | Segment | Miles |
|-----|---------|-------|
| 4 | Pecks Corner → Icewater Spring | 7.4 |
| 5 | Icewater Spring → Mt. Collins | 7.5 (+1.0 mi round trip to shelter) |
| 6 | Mt. Collins → Derrick Knob | 13.5 |
| 7 | Derrick Knob → Mollies Ridge | 11.7 |
| 8 | Mollies Ridge → Fontana Dam | 10.6 |

This turns 8 days into 9, requiring an extra night. You'd need the shelter
reserved in advance. If you already have 9 nights on the permit (Night 0
through Night 8), this works; otherwise call the Backcountry Office to adjust.

### If You Want to Split the Final Day
Use Birch Spring Gap Campsite #113:

| Day | Segment | Miles |
|-----|---------|-------|
| 7 | Derrick Knob → Mollies Ridge | 11.7 |
| 8 | Mollies Ridge → Birch Spring Gap (CS #113) | ~5.3 |
| 9 | Birch Spring Gap → Fontana Dam | ~5.3 |

Must have the campsite on your permit. This is tent-only (no shelter), so
everyone needs a tent or tarp for that night.

### Backup Shelter Swaps (Same Night, Different Shelter)
If your preferred shelter is booked when reserving, these are close substitutes:

| Preferred | Backup | Distance Difference |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Double Spring Gap | Silers Bald | 1.7 mi further (adds to Day 5, shortens Day 6) |
| Mollies Ridge | Russell Field | 2.5 mi shorter (adds 2.5 mi to final day = 13.1 mi) |
| Spence Field | Russell Field | 2.9 mi further (minimal impact) |
| Pecks Corner | (skip, extend to Icewater Spring) | Combines into 12.6 mi day |

## Parking & Vehicle Logistics

### Two-Car Shuttle Plan

1. **Day before or morning of May 18:** Drive both cars to Fontana Dam. Leave Car B
   at the Fontana Dam parking area.
2. **Drive Car A** from Fontana Dam to Davenport Gap trailhead (TN 32 / NC 284).
   This is approximately a 2.5-3 hour drive via US-129 and I-40 (about 100 miles
   depending on route).
3. **Park Car A** at Davenport Gap. Display parking tag.
4. **Hike the trail** May 18-26.
5. **May 26 afternoon:** Arrive at Fontana Dam. Pick up Car B.
6. **Drive Car B** back to Davenport Gap to retrieve Car A, or arrange a separate
   pickup depending on group logistics.

### Parking Tag Timing

- **Car B at Fontana Dam:** Parked from May 18 to May 26 = 9 days. A weekly tag
  covers 7 days, so you'll need either two weekly tags ($30) or explore an annual ($40)
  if you'll visit the park again. Alternatively, time the weekly tag to start May 19
  (since May 18 is a driving day, you might not be parked >15 min) -- verify this logic.
- **Car A at Davenport Gap:** Parked from May 18 to whenever you retrieve it after
  May 26. A weekly tag should cover this.

### Parking Areas

- **Davenport Gap (NE start):** Small gravel lot at the TN 32 / NC 284 intersection.
  Limited spaces. No kiosk. Arrive early. Some hikers park at the nearby Big Creek
  ranger station lot instead (adds a short road walk to the AT).
- **Fontana Dam (SW finish):** Larger parking area near the "Fontana Hilton" shelter
  and dam visitor center. More spaces available. No kiosk -- buy tags online.

### Drive Distance Between Trailheads
- Davenport Gap to Fontana Dam by car: ~100 miles, 2.5-3 hours
- Route: TN 32 → I-40 West → US-19 → US-129 → NC 28 → Fontana Dam Road
- Alternatively via US-441 through the park (Newfound Gap Road), but this is slower
  and may have traffic

## Water Sources

Most shelters have springs nearby, but water reliability varies by season and recent
rainfall. May is generally a wet month, so springs should be flowing, but always carry
enough water to get to the next source.

**Most reliable springs:**
- Icewater Spring (excellent -- cold and close to shelter)
- Double Spring Gap (two springs -- hence the name)
- Birch Spring Gap (if you pass through)

**Potentially less reliable in dry conditions:**
- Davenport Gap Shelter spring (lower elevation, can slow in dry spells)
- Some ridge-top shelters (Cosby Knob, Tricorner Knob) -- springs are gravity-fed
  from above and may slow if there hasn't been recent rain

**Treatment:** Filter and/or treat ALL water. The Smokies have wildlife (bears, boar,
various mammals) and the springs, while generally clean, are not guaranteed safe.
Giardia and other pathogens are possible.

**Carry capacity recommendation:** At least 2L capacity per person. On the longer
Day 5 (13.8 mi), consider carrying extra, especially if it's hot.

## Bear Protocol

Black bears are active in GSMNP year-round but especially in spring (May) when they're
emerging from winter dens and foraging heavily.

**Required at shelters:**
- Use the bear cables at every shelter to hang all food, trash, toiletries, and anything
  with an odor
- Do not store food in your pack overnight -- even inside the shelter
- Cook and eat away from the shelter/sleeping area when possible

**On the trail:**
- If you encounter a bear, do not approach. Make noise and back away slowly.
- Never run from a bear
- If a bear approaches you, stand your ground, make yourself large, and make noise
- Report aggressive bear behavior to the Backcountry Office

**Bear canisters:** Not required in GSMNP (the cable system at shelters serves this
purpose), but some hikers carry them as extra insurance.

## Newfound Gap Road Crossing

On Day 4/5, the trail crosses Newfound Gap (US-441), the only road crossing of the AT
in the Smokies. This is relevant for several reasons:

- **Resupply opportunity:** There are no stores at the gap itself, but you could
  pre-position a cooler in a car, or have someone meet you. The gap has a large
  parking lot, restrooms, and an orientation area.
- **Bail-out point:** If someone needs to leave the trail, this is the easiest
  extraction point. It's a paved road to either Gatlinburg (TN side) or Cherokee (NC
  side), both about 15-20 miles away.
- **Crowds:** Newfound Gap is the most visited spot in the park. The AT shelters
  nearby (Icewater Spring, Mt. Collins) see more traffic because of road access.
- **Elevation:** ~5,046 ft at the gap

## Emergency & Bail-Out Points

In case of injury, severe weather, or other emergency:

| Location | How to Exit | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Davenport Gap area | Walk back to TN 32 trailhead (0-8 mi) | Easiest early exit |
| Near Pecks Corner | Hughes Ridge Trail down to Smokemont (long, ~10 mi) | Not ideal |
| **Newfound Gap** | **Walk to US-441 parking lot** | **Best mid-trail exit. Paved road to towns.** |
| Near Clingmans Dome | Clingmans Dome Road to Newfound Gap (~7 mi road walk) | Seasonal road, may be closed |
| Spence Field area | Eagle Creek or other trails down, but long and remote | Not recommended unless necessary |
| Near Fontana Dam | Continue to Fontana (~5-10 mi depending on position) | Final stretch is all descent |

**Emergency number:** 911 (cell service is spotty on the ridgeline but sometimes
available at high points and near Newfound Gap)

**Non-emergency NPS dispatch:** 865-436-1230

**Backcountry Office:** 865-436-1297
