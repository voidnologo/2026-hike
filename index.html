Day-by-Day Itinerary

Night 0 · May 17

Arrival & Shuttle Setup

Arrive and camp or stay near Davenport Gap / Big Creek area (not yet inside the park). Drop one car at Fontana Dam the day before, or arrange a self-shuttle.

Day 1 · May 18

Davenport Gap to Cosby Knob Shelter

7–8 mi
▲ ~3,000' gain ▼ ~400' loss 2,000' → 4,700'

Short, manageable first day with a solid climb. Settle into the rhythm and enjoy the initial forest. Steady climb through forest; lower elevations feel milder in May. Water usually available near shelter.

Cosby Knob Shelter · sleeps 12
Day 2 · May 19

Cosby Knob to Tricorner Knob Shelter

9–10 mi
▲ ~2,350' gain ▼ ~1,100' loss 4,740' → 5,920'

High-elevation spruce-fir forest. Passes near Mt. Cammerer side trail (optional steep climb to fire tower). Some of the most remote and beautiful ridgeline walking in the northern Smokies. Potential for cooler temps and wind.

Tricorner Knob Shelter
Day 3 · May 20

Tricorner Knob to Mt. Collins Shelter

10–11 mi
▲ ~2,200' gain ▼ ~2,150' loss 5,920' → 5,900'

Passes near Pecks Corner and approaches the Newfound Gap zone. Expect more foot traffic near roads. This section stays mostly above 5,000–6,000 ft with lots of rolling terrain. Water sources generally reliable.

Mt. Collins Shelter · popular (near Newfound Gap)
Day 4 · May 21

Mt. Collins to Derrick Knob Shelter

11–12 mi
▲ ~2,750' gain ▼ ~3,750' loss 5,870' → 4,800'
⛰ Highest Point on the AT

Side trail to Kuwohi (Clingmans Dome) at 6,643 ft — the highest point on the entire Appalachian Trail (~0.5 mile round-trip for the tower and panoramic views). Significant rollercoaster with steep descents after the high point. Expect possible wind and rain at elevation. One of the tougher days.

Derrick Knob Shelter
Day 5 · May 22

Derrick Knob to Spence Field Shelter

10–11 mi
▲ ~3,000' gain ▼ ~2,650' loss 4,770' → 4,900'

Beautiful open balds and meadows — great for May wildflowers. Thunderhead Mountain and Rocky Top nearby with iconic views. Grassy areas make for nice breaks. Shelters here are popular; have backup options when booking.

Spence Field Shelter or Russell Field Shelter
Day 6 · May 23

Spence Field to Mollies Ridge Shelter

9–10 mi
▲ ~2,200' gain ▼ ~2,500' loss 4,800' → 4,570'

Transition toward the western Smokies. Some easier stretches mixed with steep rolls. Descending trend begins as you head toward lower elevations near Fontana. Nice transition day.

Mollies Ridge Shelter
Day 7 · May 24

Mollies Ridge to Birch Spring Gap

~10 mi
▲ ~2,000' gain ▼ ~2,800' loss 4,570' → 3,700'

More descending overall. Passes Doe Knob and other gaps. Water at Birch Spring Gap. Final big push through forested sections before the dam area.

Birch Spring Gap campsite
Day 8 · May 25–26

Birch Spring Gap to Fontana Dam

8–10 mi
▲ ~1,250' gain ▼ ~3,000' loss 3,680' → 1,800'

Steep final drop to the dam. The "Fontana Hilton" is just outside the park boundary. Finish early enough to retrieve your car. Celebrate the traverse!

Shelter Information

All overnight stays require advance reservations via a General Backcountry Permit. You must reserve a specific shelter for each night — no first-come, first-served for general permit holders.

Shelter Miles In Elevation Notes

Tricorner Knob ~16–18 ~5,900' High-elevation, remote feel
Mt. Collins ~26–28 ~5,900' Popular (near Newfound Gap)

Spence Field ~47–50 ~4,900' Beautiful balds; very popular in spring
Russell Field ~47–50 ~4,600' Close alternate to Spence Field

Alternate shelters: Double Spring Gap, Siler's Bald, Pecks Corner

Shelter Amenities

  • Bear cables for food storage (mandatory)
  • Nearby water sources (treat/filter all water)
  • Composting toilet privies
  • Fire rings (downed wood only)

Key Rules

  • No consecutive nights at any shelter
  • Max party size: 8
  • No tenting at shelters (general permit)
  • Print your paper permit

Parking Tags

Both shuttle vehicles need a valid parking tag while parked at the trailheads. Tags are required for any vehicle parked more than 15 minutes.

Type Cost Duration
Daily $5 1 day
Weekly $15 Up to 7 days
Annual $40 1 year

Recommendation: Buy a weekly tag ($15) for each vehicle. Purchase online at recreation.gov and print a paper copy. Display on the front lower passenger-side dashboard. Tags are non-transferable — each must list the correct license plate.

Heads up: Most remote trailheads (Davenport Gap, Big Creek, Fontana Dam) do not have kiosks — buy online to be safe. Parking is first-come, first-served; a tag does not reserve a spot.

Trail Tips

Pacing

  • Days 4 and 5 are the most strenuous
  • High elevation + big vertical swings
  • Start early on bigger mileage days
  • ~250–280 ft vertical per mile average

May Weather

  • Expect mud on the trail
  • Possible lingering snow/ice above 5,000'
  • Afternoon thunderstorms common
  • Cool nights at elevation

Water

  • Most shelters have springs nearby
  • Always treat and filter all water
  • May is generally wetter
  • Dry stretches can still occur

Bears & Food

  • Bear activity is common in May
  • Use bear cables at every shelter
  • Hang all odorous items
  • Properly store food every night
